The Sama Range

Sama, which is home to a diversity of 254 species of flora and more than 180 species of high-altitude fauna, is the main source of surface and underground water resources in the Central Valley.

Casa Dorada

La Casa Dorada or The Golden House is modernist in style, inspired by the style of the School of Fine Arts in Paris. It has been considered a national monument since April 1992. It currently functions as the House of Culture of Tarija.

Central Market

Located in center of the city, this market is considered one of the most modern in the country. It has approximately 500 merchants, dedicated to the sectors: traditional meal, dining, breads, meats, fruits, crafts, smoothies and others.

The San Jacinto Dam

Near the San Jacinto Dam and with the purpose of boosting the economy of Tarija, the San Jacinto Fairgrounds offers concerts and fairs such as The EXPOSUR that take place throughout the year.

Carnaval Chapaco

The Tarija carnival or Chapaco carnival is one of the most important celebrations in February. The streets of Tarija are dressed in celebration and music and dancing invade the entire city. During these days, parades and popular dances take place in the main square.

Viewpoint of dreams

This popular Viewpoint represents the dreams of the locals, to grow and achieve a better future. Its peculiar glass shape alludes to the most developed productive activity in the region, wine-growing work, the production of wines, grapes and singanis.
